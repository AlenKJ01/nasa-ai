Organization Overview: NASA
Full Name: National Aeronautics and Space Administration
Founded: July 29, 1958
Headquarters: Washington, D.C., USA
Type: Independent agency of the U.S. Federal Government
Purpose: To explore space, conduct scientific discovery, advance aeronautics, and develop space technologies.

Mission Statement:
NASA's mission is to pioneer the future in space exploration, scientific discovery, aeronautics research, and technological advancements for the benefit of humanity.

Vision:
To discover and expand knowledge for the betterment of humankind, while leading the world in space exploration and scientific innovation.

Core Goals:
1. Extend human presence deeper into space, including the Moon and Mars.
2. Advance scientific understanding of Earth, the solar system, and beyond.
3. Innovate in aeronautics and aviation technologies.
4. Develop cutting-edge space technologies and robotics.
5. Strengthen international collaboration in space science and exploration.

Primary Divisions:
- Human Exploration and Operations Mission Directorate (HEOMD)
- Science Mission Directorate (SMD)
- Space Technology Mission Directorate (STMD)
- Aeronautics Research Mission Directorate (ARMD)

Key Programs:
1. Artemis Program:
   A mission to return humans to the Moon, establish sustainable lunar exploration, and prepare for future crewed missions to Mars.

2. Solar System Exploration:
   Research and missions involving planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and the Sun.

3. Exoplanet Exploration:
   Study of planets beyond our solar system using telescopes like Kepler, TESS, and the James Webb Space Telescope.

4. Earth Science Program:
   Monitoring climate, weather patterns, oceans, and the environment using satellites and observational tools.

5. Mars Exploration Program:
   Includes past and ongoing rover missions such as Curiosity, Perseverance, and the Ingenuity helicopter.

6. International Space Station (ISS):
   A multinational research laboratory in low Earth orbit supporting scientific experiments across disciplines.

Notable Achievements:
- Apollo missions, landing humans on the Moon.
- Mars landers and rovers, including Perseverance.
- Hubble Space Telescope observations.
- James Webb Space Telescope discoveries.
- TESS and Kepler discoveries of thousands of exoplanets.
- Voyager spacecraft reaching interstellar space.

Organizational Values:
- Curiosity and discovery
- International collaboration
- Innovation and scientific integrity
- Advancing knowledge for humanity
